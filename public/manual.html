1. Introduction & Roles

WorkShopBar is a web-based system for managing daily bar operations — recording sales, tracking unpaid tabs, monitoring stock, and reviewing revenue. It runs on any device with a browser: phone, tablet, or computer.

Two roles

RoleWhat they can do
Owner Full access: dashboard, sales, tabs, customers, menu, inventory, reports, analytics, staff management, and voiding sales.
Bartender Record sales, view sales history, manage open tabs, and look up customers. Cannot access financial reports, inventory, or staff settings.
Note: Pages and navigation items not available to your role are hidden automatically. Bartenders will not see Dashboard, Inventory, Reports, Analytics, or Staff in their menu.

2. Logging In

  1. Open the WorkShopBar URL in any browser.
  2. Enter your name and password as provided by the owner.
  3. Tap or click Sign In.
  4. You are now on the main screen. The navigation is on the left (desktop) or accessible via the menu button at the top (mobile).
Tip: On mobile, bookmark the URL on your home screen for one-tap access.

Signing out

Tap your name at the bottom of the sidebar and press Sign Out. Always sign out when leaving a shared device.

Language

Toggle between English and Amharic using the EN / አማ buttons at the bottom of the sidebar.

4. Recording a Sale All staff

Go to New Sale from the navigation.

Step 1 — Customer (optional)

  1. Type the customer's name in the search box. Results appear as you type.
  2. Select the customer from the list, or press Create "[name]" to add them on the spot.
  3. Leave blank for an anonymous (walk-in) sale.
Existing tab alert: If the selected customer already has an open unpaid tab, a yellow banner appears showing what they already owe. You will see two options:
  • Add to existing tab — appends the new items to the same tab (recommended).
  • New tab — creates a separate new tab for this visit.

Step 2 — Items

  1. Tap any item to add it to the order. Items are grouped by category.
  2. Use the + and − buttons to adjust quantities.
  3. The running total updates automatically at the bottom.
Stock warning: If an item does not have enough stock when you try to submit, the system will reject the sale and tell you which item is short.

Step 3 — Payment

OptionUse when
Paid nowThe customer pays the full amount immediately.
Partial paymentThe customer pays part now and owes the rest. Enter the amount paid.
Open tabThe customer leaves without paying. The tab will appear in Open Tabs.
Note: When adding to an existing tab, the payment section is disabled — items are simply added to the existing tab. Collect payment from the Open Tabs page.

Step 4 — Submit

Press the Record Sale button at the bottom. You will be redirected to the sales history on success. If something goes wrong (e.g. out of stock) an error message appears.

5. Open Tabs All staff

Open Tabs lists every unpaid order, sorted oldest first (highest priority at the top). The total owed across all tabs is shown at the top right.

What each tab card shows

  • Customer name and the date/bartender who opened it.
  • Items ordered with quantities and subtotals.
  • Payment history — if the customer has made partial payments, each payment is listed with its date and amount.
  • Amount owed — for partial payments: original total (strikethrough), amount paid (green), remaining balance (yellow, bold).

Collecting payment

Partial payment

  1. Press Add Payment on the tab card.
  2. Enter the amount the customer is paying now.
  3. Press Record. The remaining balance updates immediately. The payment is logged with the current date and time.

Full payment (close the tab)

  1. Press Mark Paid on the tab card.
  2. Confirm. The tab disappears from the list. The remaining balance is recorded as a payment.
Tip: Payment history is always visible on the tab card and on the customer's profile page, so you can see exactly when and how much each customer has paid.

6. Customers All staff

The Customers page lists all registered customers. Use the search bar to find a customer by name.

Customer list

Each customer card shows:

  • Name and phone number (if recorded).
  • Total number of sales.
  • Open tab amount (if they owe anything).

Adding a customer manually

  1. Press Add Customer.
  2. Enter the name (required), phone, and any notes.
  3. Press Save.
Customers are also created automatically when you type a new name in the New Sale form.

Customer profile page

Click on any customer to open their profile. This shows:

  • Total outstanding balance across all open tabs.
  • Full sales history — every order ever recorded for this customer.
  • For each open sale: items ordered, payment history with dates, remaining balance, and buttons to add a payment or mark fully paid.

Editing a customer

Press the edit (pencil) icon on the customer's profile page to update their name, phone, or notes.

7. Sales History All staff

Sales History shows every order ever recorded. Use the filters and sort options at the top to find what you need.

Filters

  • All / Paid / Unpaid — filter by payment status.
  • Sort by — Date, Amount, or Customer name.

Sale card

Each sale shows: customer name, date, bartender who recorded it, items with quantities and prices, total amount, and payment status badge.

Voiding a sale Owner only

If a sale was recorded by mistake, owners can void it:

  1. Find the sale in Sales History.
  2. Press the Void button.
  3. Confirm. The sale is deleted and inventory is automatically restored.
Warning: Voiding is permanent. The sale is removed from all reports and the stock is returned. Use only for genuine recording mistakes.

8. Inventory Management Owner only

Inventory tracks the physical stock of every item you sell. Stock levels decrease automatically when a sale is recorded.

Adding a new item to inventory

  1. Press Track Item.
  2. Fill in the form:
    • Name — the name that appears on the menu (e.g. "Heineken").
    • Price — selling price per unit.
    • Category — select an existing category or leave blank.
    • Starting Quantity — how many units you have right now.
    • Unit — e.g. bottles, cans, pints.
    • Low Stock Alert at — the quantity at which the item should be flagged as low.
  3. Press Add. The item is created in inventory and added to the menu automatically.

Stock levels table

Status badgeMeaning
OK (green)Stock is above the low threshold.
LOW (yellow)Stock is at or below the low threshold. Restock soon.
OUT (red)Zero stock. Selling this item is blocked until stock is added.

Adjusting stock

When you receive a delivery or need to correct a quantity:

  1. Find the item and press Adjust Stock.
  2. Enter the new total quantity (not the amount to add — enter the final number).
  3. Press Save.

Removing an item

Press the delete icon on an item. If the item has no sales history it is permanently deleted. If it has sales history it is deactivated (hidden from the menu but history is preserved).

9. Menu Management Owner only

The Menu page shows all active menu items available for sale. Items are created primarily through Inventory (see section 8), which creates both the menu item and the stock tracking record at once.

Editing an item

  1. Find the item and press the edit (pencil) icon.
  2. Update the name, price, or category.
  3. Press Save.
Note: Changing a price only affects future sales. All past sales retain the price that was set at the time of the order.

Deactivating / Deleting an item

  • Deactivate — hides the item from the sale form but keeps all history. Use this when an item is temporarily unavailable.
  • Delete — permanently removes items with no sales history. Items with sales history are deactivated instead.

10. Staff Management Owner only

Each member of staff should have their own login account so that every sale is linked to who recorded it.

Adding a new staff account

  1. Go to Staff in the navigation.
  2. Press Add Staff.
  3. Enter the staff member's name, a password (minimum 4 characters), and select their role (Bartender or Owner).
  4. Press Add. Give them their password privately.
Best practice: Use each bartender's first name as their username so sales reports clearly show who sold what.

Removing a staff account

  1. Find the staff member in the list.
  2. Press the delete (trash) icon.
  3. Confirm. The account is removed. Their past sales records are preserved.
Note: You cannot delete your own account. If you need to remove an owner account, log in as a different owner first.

Changing a password

Currently, to change a password you must remove the account and create a new one with the same name and new password.

11. Reports & Analytics Owner only

Reports page

Shows revenue over time and the top-selling items.

  • Daily view — revenue by hour for today.
  • Weekly view — revenue by day for the last 7 days.
  • Top items — the 8 most-sold items by quantity in the selected period.

Analytics page

Longer-range revenue and sales count trends:

  • Last 30 Days — daily breakdown.
  • Last 12 Months — monthly breakdown.
  • All Time (by Year) — yearly totals.

How revenue is counted

Revenue figures count cash received on the day it was collected — not when the order was placed. This means:

  • A tab opened Monday and paid Friday will appear in Friday's revenue.
  • A partial payment on Wednesday will appear in Wednesday's revenue.
  • This gives an accurate picture of when money actually came into the bar.

12. Quick Reference

Daily workflow — Bartender

  1. Log in at the start of your shift.
  2. For each order: go to New Sale, select the customer (or leave blank), add items, choose paid/partial/open tab, and submit.
  3. When a customer with an open tab orders again: select their name — a yellow banner will appear. Choose Add to existing tab to keep everything on one tab.
  4. When a customer settles their tab: go to Open Tabs, press Add Payment for partial payments or Mark Paid to close the tab.
  5. Sign out at the end of your shift.

Daily workflow — Owner

  1. Check the Dashboard to see today's revenue, paid vs unpaid totals, and any low-stock alerts.
  2. Review Open Tabs to see which customers owe money.
  3. Use Inventory to adjust stock after restocking deliveries.
  4. Use Reports or Analytics to review weekly/monthly performance.
